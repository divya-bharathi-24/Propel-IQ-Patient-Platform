# User Story - US_011

## Story ID

- ID: US_011

## Story Title

- Patient Self-Registration with Email Verification

## Description

- As a new patient, I want to create an account by providing my name, email, phone number, date of birth, and password with email verification, so that I can securely access the platform after confirming my identity.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. **Given** I am on the registration page, **When** I enter my name, email, phone number, date of birth, and a password meeting complexity requirements (8+ characters, mixed case, number, special character), **Then** the system creates my account in a pending state and sends a verification email within 2 minutes.

2. **Given** I receive the verification email, **When** I click the verification link, **Then** the system activates my account, redirects me to the login page, and displays a success message.

3. **Given** I am registering, **When** I enter an email already associated with an existing account, **Then** the system displays an error message indicating the email is already registered without revealing account details.

4. **Given** I am registering, **When** my password does not meet complexity requirements, **Then** the system displays specific password requirements (8+ characters, one uppercase, one number, one special character) and prompts re-entry.

5. **Given** I received a verification email, **When** I click the link after 24 hours have passed, **Then** the system displays "Link expired" and offers to resend a new verification email.

6. **Given** my password is submitted, **When** the system stores it, **Then** the password is hashed using bcrypt or Argon2 and never stored in plaintext.

## Edge Cases

- What happens when a patient requests multiple verification emails? (Rate limit: max 3 verification emails per hour per email address)
- How does the system handle registration with a disposable email domain? (Accept all valid email formats; email verification ensures deliverability)
